Petrarchan Sonnets

All That Glitters Is Beloved

By Taylor Laurent

(Zaki Leaño)

Your hair shines beneath the moonlight’s gentle touch.

It flows like the tranquil waves of a calm silken sea.

Every lock of golden thread falls into place perfectly.

Your pale skin’s clear, your cheeks warm with a rosy blush

I dislike how men would kill to have you in their clutch

You’re the culmination of what people call ‘pretty’

Hate how every time you say ‘hi’ my heart starts to flee

Falling feels fun until you hit the ground and your bones crush

People push and shove, fighting for your gaze

A precious person they want for themselves

Gunning for the gold, for your loving praise

They’d do anything for a whiff of your sweet smell

And me? I’m here, drowning in malaise

Jealous of your beauty in my self-made hell

My Love, My Essence

By Diana Bercero

(Thiana Desabille)

Your face once said it all

It told me more than cliched words ever could

Now we part, each doing what we should

Nothing more I will do but let this love forestall

I’ve seen you turn away, like a fleeting song

I truly wish to know if these words are so burdensome

That you can’t spare a moment, though I can spare more than some

I sit with you this eve sensing it won’t be long

Tell me truthfully, was it a dream, or a guise?

I know you are not mine

So leave me, cast me aside

May God grace you, without any disguise

Although I love you, I’ll be fine

Still, I love you, it can’t be denied

Ever Pure Being With You

By Caroline Qimson

(Corinne Roldan)

Purify me, gorgeous angel

Bring the flaws out from my bloodstream

Until there's nothing left but a distant scream

Oh disperse me, soft-hearted faithful,

Wear down my gratefulness until it's painful

You kissed me for what I am, not for some scheme

Being with you in that moment was just like a dream

It was simple, tender, and not the least bit baneful

You’re perfection and distinction

I’ve no qualms about your nonchalance

If I said limiting words with conviction

You’d come and give me your sweet response

Our lips touched, not for some sorrowful fiction

Shakesperean Sonnets

Empty Spot, Still Warm

By Taylor Laurent

(Zaki Leaño)

You warned me to never love so easy and high,

That I'd let myself be used and forget my smarts.

You warned all the same to never be so cunning and sly,

That I'd be swallowed by evil and forget my heart.

I remember the bitter morning chill that roused me from my slumber,

The warmth of your love for the amber autumn skies fought the cold,

We'd find the lake, we'd bathe in the freezing reflected umber,

Always would we go past where our feet could hold.

I should have never always complained about the long walks to that lake,

I didn't know every single scrap of you would be stolen from me.

But you live through your words, ‘There's always give after every take’,

As well as a little box of dreams I inherited from thee.

What had wilted in my arms didn't fully yet return to dirt and ashes

I believe we're back at the lake making laughter and splashes.

Melody of Remorse

By Diana Bercero

(Thiana Desabille)

Alongside the shore, a girl stands alone

She looks into the water, staring, yearning

It is here, by the coast, she keeps some things unknown

A secret ‘bout something so concerning

“Amidst the bleakest of times, you consistently stood by me

Regardless of my words, you stayed

I allowed my greed to grow to such a degree

That I brought upon you a world of dismay

Now, entirely because of my faults

You are no more by my side

To relieve the stresses from my heart’s vaults

I must pour my sorrows into the tide”

With tear-filled eyes, she rues that bygone age

“If the waves sway, in rebirth’s play, may we both be on the same stage”

Less Than Human, More Than Dirt

By Caroline Qimson

(Corinne Roldan)

God knows the gravity of my debt

And I know how much it can't be repaid

Cause I'm not sure what non-deviants want from crushed up sidewalk cigarettes

And nighttime people you pay, still with a world between us, beside you I stayed

Carefully out of reach near the edge of the couch

Less precious than uncommon, less vintage than old

No chances to be more than just the burden that makes you slouch

I swear stagnancy's for the frosted and cold

But I see no logic in someone loving a pest

I swear there’s an attempt in the making

Apologies and promises I’m doing my best

But there’s no humanity to be found in what I am faking

If you questioned me, everything would fall apart

And all you'd find is my inhuman heart
